Malaria Testing Device Without Blood.

  • Thomas Johnson
  • August 8, 2017

A non-invasive device that uses red light and a custom-made sensor to detect malaria from a patient’s finger, eliminating the need for blood samples. Won the Africa Prize for Engineering Innovation..

Eco-Friendly Bricks from Plastic Waste.

  • Thomas Johnson
  • August 8, 2017

She developed lightweight and strong bricks using melted plastic and sand, providing an affordable and eco-conscious solution for housing.

Remote-Controlled Irrigation for Small Farmers.

  • Thomas Johnson
  • August 8, 2017

A mobile-controlled water irrigation system that helps farmers in dry areas save water and grow more crops efficiently using sensors and weather data.

Solar Bag for Charging and Lighting.

  • Thomas Johnson
  • August 8, 2017

Designed backpacks fitted with small solar panels to help rural students charge lamps for evening reading. The bag also provides mobile phone charging..

Interactive Science Experiments Platform.

  • Thomas Johnson
  • August 8, 2017

A mobile and web-based platform that lets secondary school students simulate science experiments without needing expensive lab equipment..

AI-Powered Traffic Lights.

  • Thomas Johnson
  • August 8, 2017

Designed intelligent traffic lights that respond to real-time traffic density, aiming to reduce road congestion in Kampala city.
